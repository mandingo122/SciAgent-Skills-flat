# Scientific Talk Types Guide

## Overview

Different presentation contexts require different approaches, structures, and emphasis. This guide provides detailed guidance for common scientific talk types.

## Conference Talks (10–20 min)

### Context
- **Duration**: 10–20 minutes (15 most common)
- **Audience**: Mix of specialists and non-specialists
- **Setting**: Concurrent sessions, audience may arrive late
- **Goal**: Communicate key findings, generate interest, network
- **Format**: Often followed by 2–5 minutes of questions

### Structure for 15-Minute Conference Talk

**Recommended Slide Count**: 15–18 slides

```
Introduction (2–3 min, 2–3 slides):
├─ Title + hook (30 sec)
├─ Background and significance (90 sec)
└─ Research question (60 sec)

Methods (2–3 min, 2–3 slides):
├─ Study design overview
├─ Key methodological approach
└─ Analysis strategy

Results (6–7 min, 6–8 slides):
├─ Primary finding (2–3 min, 2–3 slides)
├─ Secondary finding (2 min, 2 slides)
└─ Additional validation (2 min, 2–3 slides)

Discussion (2–3 min, 3–4 slides):
├─ Interpretation
├─ Comparison to prior work
├─ Implications
└─ Limitations

Conclusion (1 min, 1–2 slides):
├─ Key takeaways
└─ Acknowledgments
```

### Best Practices

**Opening**: Start with attention-grabbing hook (surprising fact, compelling image). Clearly state why this work matters. Preview main finding early. Don't spend >2 min on background.

**Content**: Focus on 1–2 key findings. Use compelling visuals. Show data, not just conclusions. Don't include every analysis from paper.

**Delivery**: Practice exact timing. Make eye contact. End with clear, memorable conclusion. Never run over time.

**Q&A**: Prepare backup slides with extra data. Anticipate questions. Keep answers to 30–60 seconds.

### Lightning Talks (5–7 min)

```
Slide 1: Title (15 sec)
Slide 2: The Problem (45 sec)
Slide 3: Your Approach (60 sec)
Slide 4–5: Key Result (2–3 min)
Slide 6: Impact/Implications (45 sec)
Slide 7: Conclusion + Contact (30 sec)
```

ONE main message only. Maximize visuals, minimize text. Practice exact timing rigorously.

### Poster Spotlight Talks (3 min)

**Purpose**: Drive traffic to poster session.

```
1 slide: Title + Context (30 sec)
2 slides: Problem + Approach (60 sec)
2 slides: Most Interesting Result (60 sec)
1 slide: "Visit my poster at #42" (30 sec)
```

Show teaser, not full story. Include poster number prominently. Use QR code for details.

## Academic Seminars (45–60 min)

### Context
- **Duration**: 45–60 minutes
- **Audience**: Department faculty, students, postdocs
- **Setting**: Single presentation, full attention
- **Goal**: Deep dive into research, get feedback, show expertise
- **Format**: Extended Q&A (10–15 min), interruptions welcome

### Structure for 50-Minute Seminar

**Recommended Slide Count**: 40–50 slides

```
Introduction (8–10 min, 8–10 slides):
├─ Personal introduction (1 min)
├─ Big picture context (3–4 min)
├─ Literature review (3–4 min)
├─ Research questions (1–2 min)
└─ Roadmap/outline (1 min)

Methods (8–10 min, 8–10 slides):
├─ Study design with rationale (2–3 min)
├─ Participants/materials (2 min)
├─ Procedures (3–4 min)
└─ Analysis approach (2 min)

Results (18–22 min, 16–20 slides):
├─ Overview/demographics (2 min)
├─ Main finding 1 (6–8 min)
├─ Main finding 2 (6–8 min)
├─ Additional analyses (4–6 min)
└─ Summary slide (1 min)

Discussion (10–12 min, 8–10 slides):
├─ Summary of findings (2 min)
├─ Relation to literature (3–4 min)
├─ Mechanisms/explanations (2–3 min)
├─ Limitations (2 min)
└─ Implications (2 min)

Conclusion (2–3 min, 2–3 slides):
├─ Key messages (1 min)
├─ Future directions (1–2 min)
└─ Acknowledgments (30 sec)
```

### Best Practices

**Engagement**: Welcome interruptions. Use checkpoint questions ("Does this make sense?"). Admit what you don't know. Ask audience for input.

**Content**: Go deeper into methods than conference talk. Discuss failed experiments and pivots. Present ongoing/unpublished work. Connect to broader theoretical questions.

**Job Talk Additions**: Show research program trajectory (past → present → future). Demonstrate independent thinking. Explain funding strategy. Add "Future Directions" section (5 min).

## Thesis and Dissertation Defenses (30–60 min)

### Context
- **Duration**: 30–60 minutes (varies by institution)
- **Audience**: Committee, colleagues, family
- **Setting**: Formal examination
- **Goal**: Demonstrate mastery, defend research decisions
- **Format**: Extended Q&A (30–90 min), private or public

### Structure for 45-Minute Defense

**Recommended Slide Count**: 40–50 slides

```
Introduction (5 min, 5–6 slides):
├─ Research context and motivation
├─ Central thesis question
├─ Overview of studies/chapters
└─ Roadmap

Literature Review (5 min, 4–5 slides):
├─ Theoretical framework
├─ Key prior findings
├─ Knowledge gaps
└─ Your contribution

Study 1 (8–10 min, 10–12 slides):
├─ Research question
├─ Methods
├─ Results
└─ Interim conclusions

Study 2 (8–10 min, 10–12 slides): [same structure]

Study 3 (optional) (8–10 min, 10–12 slides): [same structure]

General Discussion (8–10 min, 8–10 slides):
├─ Synthesis across studies
├─ Theoretical implications
├─ Practical applications
├─ Limitations (comprehensive)
└─ Future research directions

Conclusions (2–3 min, 2–3 slides):
├─ Main contributions
├─ Final thoughts
└─ Acknowledgments
```

### Best Practices

**Preparation**: Practice 5+ times. Anticipate every possible question. Prepare backup slides. Review key literature thoroughly.

**Q&A**: Listen carefully to full question. Pause before answering. Admit when you don't know. Engage with criticism constructively.

**Handling Difficult Questions**:
- **Critique of methods**: Acknowledge limitation, explain rationale, note in future work
- **Alternative interpretations**: "That's an interesting perspective. I focused on X because..."
- **Why didn't you do X?**: "That would be valuable. Due to [constraint], I prioritized..."

## Grant Pitches (10–20 min)

### Context
- **Duration**: 10–20 minutes
- **Audience**: Funding panel, non-specialists, decision-makers
- **Setting**: Evaluative, competitive
- **Goal**: Secure funding, demonstrate feasibility and impact

### Structure for 15-Minute Grant Pitch

```
Significance (3–4 min, 3–4 slides):
├─ Problem statement with impact (90 sec)
├─ Current state and limitations (90 sec)
└─ Opportunity and innovation (60–90 sec)

Approach (5–6 min, 5–6 slides):
├─ Overall strategy (60 sec)
├─ Aim 1 (90 sec)
├─ Aim 2 (90 sec)
├─ Aim 3 (optional, 90 sec)
└─ Timeline and milestones (60 sec)

Impact and Feasibility (4–5 min, 3–4 slides):
├─ Preliminary data (2 min)
├─ Expected impact (1 min)
├─ Team and resources (1 min)
└─ Alternative strategies for risks (60 sec)

Conclusion (1 min, 1 slide):
└─ Summary of innovation and impact
```

**Key**: Lead with impact. Show feasibility with preliminary data. Connect to funder's mission.

## Journal Club Presentations (20–45 min)

### Context
- **Duration**: 20–45 minutes
- **Audience**: Lab members, colleagues, students
- **Goal**: Understand paper, critique methods, discuss implications
- **Format**: Heavy Q&A, interactive discussion

### Structure for 30-Minute Journal Club

```
Context (2–3 min, 2–3 slides):
├─ Paper citation and authors
├─ Why you chose this paper
└─ Background and significance

Introduction (3–4 min, 2–3 slides):
├─ Research question
├─ Prior work and gaps
└─ Hypotheses

Methods (5–7 min, 4–6 slides):
├─ Study design
├─ Participants/materials
├─ Procedures and analysis
└─ Your assessment of methods

Results (8–10 min, 5–7 slides):
├─ Main findings
├─ Key figures explained
├─ Statistical results
└─ Your interpretation

Discussion (5–7 min, 3–4 slides):
├─ Authors' interpretation
├─ Strengths and limitations
└─ Implications for field

Critical Analysis (3–5 min, 1–2 slides):
├─ What did we learn?
├─ What questions remain?
└─ Relevance to our work
```

**Key**: Be fair but critical. Highlight key figures. Facilitate discussion with open-ended questions.

## Summary: Choosing the Right Approach

| Talk Type | Duration | Audience | Depth | Key Focus |
|-----------|----------|----------|-------|-----------|
| Lightning | 5–7 min | General | Minimal | One key finding |
| Conference | 15 min | Specialists | Moderate | Main results |
| Seminar | 45–60 min | Experts | Deep | Comprehensive |
| Defense | 45–60 min | Committee | Complete | All studies |
| Grant | 15–20 min | Mixed | Moderate | Impact & feasibility |
| Journal Club | 30–45 min | Lab group | Critical | Methods & interpretation |

### Adaptation Checklist

- [ ] Who is my audience? (Expertise level, background, expectations)
- [ ] How much time do I have? (Strictly enforced or flexible?)
- [ ] What is the goal? (Inform, persuade, teach, impress?)
- [ ] What format is expected? (Formal vs. interactive, Q&A style)
- [ ] What will happen afterward? (Q&A, discussion, evaluation, networking)
- [ ] What are the logistics? (Room size, A/V setup, recording, remote?)
